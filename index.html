PANDIYAS HISTORY IN ONE LINE


The history of the Pandyan kingdom is typically divided into three eras, stretching from at least the 3rd century BCE to the 17th century CE. First is that of the Early or Medieval Pandyas, second the Imperial Pandyas, and third the Tenkasi Pandyas. The first two periods are the most prolific, and the Pandya kingdom reached the height of its rule during the Imperial period in the 13th century, then fell into decline from the 14-17th centuries CE.


PANDIYAN ARCHITECTURE


The Pandyas made great contributions to the literary history of India, and were also known for their sculptural abilities, perhaps they are most iconically known for their temple architecture. The Pandyas contributed to the development of Dravidian architecture, which is broadly considered as the style of temple architecture characteristic of southern India. Emblematic of Dravidian temple architecture are pyramid-like towers. These can be carved out of stone or can be constructed like other common structures.
